Towards Clean Air in Delhi Or Dangerous December: Past or Future


Towards Clean Air in Delhi Or Dangerous December: Past or Future

Organized by CERCA, IITD in association with CPCB, HLTF and EPCA

Agenda
  1. Review current status/progress of various measures/air quality improvement plans in general, readiness for air pollution abetment in winter of 2018 in particular
  2. Way Forward: Suggested Steps to be taken for a better Air Quality in Delhi
Objectives and Expected Outcome
  1. Develop, converge on pollution reduction Targets (of pollution levels) and Deadlines
  2. Develop and submit Policy Recommendations for a) Immediate (Dec 2018), b) Long Term Impact
  3. Develop Advisory Council/Partnerships to ensure follow-up of the recommendations
Approach

Work with the decision makers to make it happen! A cleaner Dec 2018 and increasingly cleaner future

About Cerca

As per a WHO survey of 1600 world cities, the air quality of Delhi and National Capital Region (NCR) has recently been ranked as the most polluted city in the world. Air pollution is now clearly recognised as a world‘s major risk factor for many deadly diseases as a result of the combined effects of different kinds of air pollutants upon the physical and mental health of people and the quality of urban life in general. Especially, urban air pollution due to vehicular emissions, industrial growth and household pollution is a matter of concern now because of the harmful exposure on the urban population.
