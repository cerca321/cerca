Objectives

Our Aim


To study the Air Pollution Reduction Programs, governance and its implementation issues in China, US, UK, Japan, South Korea, etc.

While India continues to suffer – and increasingly so – from air pollution, a number of countries have had remarkable success in addressing this problem at both the macro-scale as well as within particularly troublesome hot-spots. This project will aim to study the experiences of some major countries/regions – United States, United Kingdom, Europe (LRTAP), Japan, and China – to understand effective practices in terms of both design of policies (i.e., choice of instruments – regulations, market instruments, information disclosure, etc.) and their implementation. The intention is to see how the best of these practices may be relevant and adaptable to the Indian context. At the same time, we will also examine the feasibility of emerging policy instruments such as incentive prizes to address this problem in India. We also will examine the possible synergies (and tensions) between air pollution and climate change policies, which takes on great salience given the enormous focus being placed on reorienting the energy and agriculture sectors to mitigate climate change.

Lastly we will explore the possibility of international cooperation (technology and finance) to address air pollution in India.
