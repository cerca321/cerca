Project Details

Know more about the project


Assessment and prediction of the air-quality using dynamically downscaled high resolution data from numerical models.

PI: Prof. Vimlesh Pant, Centre for Atmospheric Sciences, IIT Delhi

Co-PIs: Prof. Sandeep Sahany, Prof. Saroj Kanta Mishra, Prof. S. K. Dash, Prof. Sandeep Sukumaran, Prof. Maithili Sharan [Centre for Atmospheric Sciences, IIT Delhi]

Synopsis:

The recently awarded ‘DST’s Centre of Excellence in Climate Modelling’ (CoE-CM) at IIT Delhi engaged in climate research with a focus on development of an India-centric climate model with least biases in meteorological parameters over the Indian region. Apart from the sources of pollutants, the air-quality over a location largely depends on the prevailing meteorological conditions. The atmospheric variables such as wind speed, wind direction, air temperature, relative humidity, surface temperature, soil moisture, boundary layer height, etc. play crucial role in determining the advection or accumulation of air-pollutants over a region. Accurate information of meteorological variables is, therefore, important to examine the air-quality over a given region. We would utilize the model data generated in the CoE-CM with the dynamical downscaling approach to address the air-quality assessment at fine regional scale over various parts of the country. Detailed analysis of the available observational data from air-quality monitoring stations and satellites would be used together with the numerical models for a better prediction and assessment of the air-quality.

Deliverables:

1. High spatial (1 km) and temporal (hourly) data of meteorological variables

2. Air-quality maps at a spatial resolution of about 1 km using numerical models and observational data.

3. Relationship between the satellite measurements and ground based observations of air pollutants.

4. Prediction of air-quality a week in advance over a desired state or region, e.g. Delhi NCR.

5. Understanding the change in the atmospheric boundary layer and its role in pollution severity.

6. Recommendations (based on our modelling study) for the planning of new/additional air-quality monitoring stations.

About Cerca

As per a WHO survey of 1600 world cities, the air quality of Delhi and National Capital Region (NCR) has recently been ranked as the most polluted city in the world. Air pollution is now clearly recognised as a world‘s major risk factor for many deadly diseases as a result of the combined effects of different kinds of air pollutants upon the physical and mental health of people and the quality of urban life in general. Especially, urban air pollution due to vehicular emissions, industrial growth and household pollution is a matter of concern now because of the harmful exposure on the urban population.
